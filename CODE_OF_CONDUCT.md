# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in the git-mosaic community a harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible
disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression,
level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive community include:

- demonstrating empathy and kindness;
- respecting differing opinions, experiences, and boundaries;
- giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback;
- taking responsibility, apologizing, and learning from mistakes;
- focusing on what is best for the community and its users;
- handling vulnerability reports and private data discreetly.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, sexualized language or attention,
trolling or insulting comments, threats, deliberate intimidation, publishing
others' private information, exposing credentials or private repository data,
and other conduct reasonably considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code applies in project spaces and whenever an individual officially
represents the project, including repositories, issue trackers, review threads,
community forums, and public events.

## Enforcement

Report abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior privately to the
project maintainers. If no private conduct-reporting address is published, use a
trusted private channel to ask a maintainer for one; do not include sensitive
details in a public issue. Security vulnerabilities should follow
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

Maintainers must respect the privacy and safety of reporters. They may remove or
edit content and may warn, temporarily restrict, or permanently ban participants
whose behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.
Enforcement decisions should consider context, impact, intent, prior behavior,
and the safety of affected people.
